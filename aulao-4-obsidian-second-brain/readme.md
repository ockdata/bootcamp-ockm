# Second Brain Vault

This vault now follows a simple top-level structure oriented around capture, execution, and reusable knowledge.

## Layout

- `0 - Staging/`: temporary drop zone for files, attachments, clips, and anything that still needs processing
- `1 - Stakeholders/`: durable notes about people, organizations, teams, clients, and partners
- `2 - Projects/`: active and archived project notes, deliverables, plans, and status
- `3 - Knowledge/`: evergreen knowledge, frameworks, concepts, and synthesized insights
- `4 - Meetings/`: meeting records, agendas, decision logs, and follow-ups
- `5 - References/`: supporting source material already processed into notes
- `6 - Notes/`: fast capture area for working notes before they are promoted elsewhere
- `skills/`: local agent skills that help organize, summarize, and maintain the vault

## Obsidian Defaults

- New notes should land in `6 - Notes/`.
- Attachments and imported files should land in `0 - Staging/`.
- `index.md` stays in the root as the vault catalog.

## Suggested Flow

1. Capture quickly in `6 - Notes/` or drop material into `0 - Staging/`.
2. Convert durable relationship context into `1 - Stakeholders/`.
3. Move execution context into `2 - Projects/` and `4 - Meetings/`.
4. Promote reusable insights into `3 - Knowledge/`.
5. Store processed supporting material in `5 - References/`.

## Maintenance Principle

Keep notes short, link related material with `[[wikilinks]]`, and prefer promoting useful content into the right folder instead of letting everything accumulate in staging or scratch notes.

## Local Skills

- `skills/second-brain-inbox-triage/`: routes new captures from staging and notes into the correct folders
- `skills/meeting-distillation/`: turns rough meeting notes into structured records and follow-ups
- `skills/project-knowledge-gardener/`: keeps project notes linked to stakeholders, references, and evergreen knowledge
